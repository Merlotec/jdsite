Senior Duke Help


Welcome to the Senior Duke help page. Here you will find information to help you navigate the Senior Duke Portal. Senior Duke is a fully online experience, so you and your pupils need to be properly registered to take part.


The Senior Duke Portal

After contacting the Senior Duke administration team, you will be sent an email to sign up and create a school administrator account. Once this is done, you can login and access your school's online portal by logging in here. Once logged in, in the upper left of the screen, you will see three horizontal tabs:

  • The Pupils tab contains the complete list of pupils who are taking part in Senior Duke and registered for an account.
  • The Teachers tab contains the complete list of teacher accounts who are able to view and mark the pupil's activities.
  • The Unreviewed Sections tab contains a list of pupil activities which need marked by a teacher.


Registering Pupils

Pupil Credits

In order to register pupils you need Pupil Credits. Each pupil credit will allow you to create a pupil account. To get pupil credits, contact the Senior Duke administration team. You will then be required to pay for the number of pupil credits required. To check how many pupil credits your school has, click on the Pupils tab and to the right of the search bar in the middle, you will see a small bubble with the number of pupil credits displayed.

Create a Pupil Account

If your school has pupil credits remaining, you can create a pupil account by navigating to the Pupils tab then clicking the Add Pupil button next to the search bar in the middle. Fill in the details for the pupil, ensuring that the email is correct. Make sure to select the correct award (Gold or Silver) from the award drop down box, then click Add Pupil at the bottom. Once this is done, the pupil will be emailed with an auto-generated password to allow them to log in and start completing their award. You can quickly create another pupil account by following the prompt on the screen.

Viewing a Pupil Account/Password

Once a pupil is registered, you may view their account by navigating to the Pupils tab then clicking on the email address of the pupil from the list of pupils. Here you can see various details about the pupil. If the pupil has not changed their password, you will be able to see their auto-generated password here as well. However if they have changed it, you should use the password reset feature to gain access to the account, if they have forgotten their password.


Registering Teachers

Teacher accounts are able to view and mark pupil activities. Only school administrator accounts are allowed to create teacher accounts - other teacher accounts cannot. To create a teacher account navigate to the Teachers tab and click on the Add Teacher button next to the search bar in the middle. Fill in the details for the teacher, ensuring the email is correct, then click Add Teacher at the bottom. Once this is done, the teacher will be emailed with an auto-generated password to allow them to log. This password can be changed by the teacher.


Reviewing Sections

Once a pupil has completed a section, they should submit it for review. Once this is done, the section will appear in the list of unreviewed sections in the Unreviewed Sections tab. You can review the section by clicking on the section row in the list. If you are happy that the section has been completed to an adequate level, click on Mark as Complete in the feedback section at the bottom. If not, provide a reason as to what to fix, then click Mark as Not Approved in the feedback section at the bottom. If you mark as complete by accident, you can click undo by scrolling back down to the bottom and clicking the Undo button. Alternatively, if you think that the pupil has gone above and beyond, you can mark the section as oustanding, which means it may be reviewed by the Senior Duke team.

You can also review any pupil's progress, even if they have not marked the section as complete. To do this, navigate to the Pupils tab then click on a the pupil that you would like to view from the list of pupils. You will then be presented with a grid of sections, identical to what the pupil themselves see when they log in. You can click on any section and review their progress, however you may not edit any part of the page.
